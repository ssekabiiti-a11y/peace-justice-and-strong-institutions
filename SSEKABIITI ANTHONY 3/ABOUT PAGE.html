Peace

Peace is a calm and happy feeling when people get along well with each other. When everyone in a place—a school, a town, or even the whole world—tries to be:

  • Kind
  • Respectful
  • Fair

Peace isn't just about not fighting—it's about working together, listening to each other, and making sure everyone feels safe and respected.

Justice

Justice means fair rules that make sure everyone is treated equally. It's when people are given what they deserve, no matter who they are. For example, if you do something wrong, justice gives you a fair chance to fix it—not just punishment without understanding.

Justice is about honesty, respecting others' rights, and ensuring everyone has a fair chance to succeed. It's like when a teacher gives everyone a chance to answer or a doctor treats all patients with care and respect.

Strong Institutions

Strong institutions are like the "rules" and "leaders" that help a community run well. They ensure things are fair and safe. For example, the government is a strong institution because it helps make laws, protect people, and treat everyone fairly.

Schools are also strong institutions. They teach students, help them grow, and make sure everyone learns equally.

Police and courts are strong institutions too. They help keep people safe and make sure rules are followed. Strong institutions help create a safe, fair, and cooperative society for all.
